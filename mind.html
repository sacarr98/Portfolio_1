Mindfullness

Mindfulness is the practice of purposely bringing one's attention to the present-moment experience without evaluation. Below you will find three mindfullness practices you can try at home.

Doodling

  1. Grab a small sheet of paper (A5 or less)
  2. Put on some music to get you into the right headspace. Pick a starting point on your page, this could be placing a single dot in the centre and working out, or from a corner to the middle.
  3. You can draw simple lines or shapes, the pattern doesn't have to be perfect, let your drawing go wherever your mind takes you.
  4. Keep going until you feel finished. It's okay if you lose focus and come back later. The more you practice the longer you'll be able to go.

Body Awareness Walk

  1. As you being walking bring awareness to the soles of you feet. How does the ground feel beneath them?
  2. After a few minutes shift the attention to your ankless and calves. How do they feel? Tired? Strong?
  3. Then shift your attention to your upper legs. Become aware of the muscles working with each step.
  4. Now become aware of the movements and sensations in your hips. Do they way as you walk? Or are they rigid?
  5. You can then raise your attention to your torso, feel each breath and the rhythm of your heartbeat.
  6. After a few minutes, begin to become aware of your shoulders, relax them down with each step.
  7. Lastly notice the way your head moves up and down with the rhythm of your steps.
  8. Continue to scan your body for the duration of your walk, focusing your attention on different parts of your body as you feel.
